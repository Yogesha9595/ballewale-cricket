Bengal resumed their innings on the second day of the match at Eden Gardens, with Gharami at the crease. His innings was marked by a blend of aggression and patience, as he struck 25 boundaries and 2 sixes, demonstrating his ability to rotate the strike while also punishing loose deliveries. Gharami's partnership with the middle order was pivotal, as he built crucial stands that allowed Bengal to extend their lead significantly. With this performance, he has firmly established himself as a key player for Bengal in the current season.

Bengal's total reached 450 runs, giving them a substantial lead over Assam, who struggled to find breakthroughs against Gharami's solid defense and attacking flair.
