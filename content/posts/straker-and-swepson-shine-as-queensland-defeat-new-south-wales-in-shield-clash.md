This match, which concluded on October 15, 2023, showcased the depth of talent in Australian domestic cricket and could serve as a preview for the upcoming Sheffield Shield final.

His ability to generate pace and movement off the pitch proved crucial, particularly on a surface that offered assistance to seamers. Straker's efforts were complemented by Swepson, who took three wickets in the first innings and provided vital breakthroughs when New South Wales appeared to be building momentum. Together, their combined efforts restricted the home side to a modest total, ultimately leading to Queensland's innings victory.

New South Wales, who started the match as favorites, struggled to find their rhythm against a disciplined Queensland bowling attack. Despite a resilient 75 from captain Kurtis Patterson, the team could not recover from early setbacks. Their first innings total of 210 was insufficient, and Queensland capitalized on the advantage, scoring 350 in their first innings. The pressure mounted on New South Wales in the second innings, where they were bowled out for just 145, handing Queensland a significant victory by an innings and 15 runs.
