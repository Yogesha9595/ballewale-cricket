In a historic achievement for Jammu & Kashmir cricket, Mohammad Nabi's outstanding bowling performance propelled the team to their maiden Ranji Trophy semi-final, defeating Madhya Pradesh by a convincing margin. Nabi's remarkable 12-wicket haul across both innings was instrumental in sealing the victory, marking a significant milestone for the team in the prestigious domestic competition.

The match, held at the iconic Gaddafi Stadium, saw Jammu & Kashmir put on a resilient display after winning the toss and opting to bat first. Despite a shaky start, they managed to post a competitive total of 250 runs in their first innings. Madhya Pradesh, in response, struggled against the relentless bowling attack led by Nabi, who claimed six wickets for just 45 runs, skittling them out for a mere 180 runs.

In their second innings, Jammu & Kashmir set a challenging target for Madhya Pradesh, declaring at 280 runs. Nabi continued to wreak havoc with the ball, claiming another six wickets, leaving the opposition reeling at 150 all out. His exceptional performance not only showcased his skills but also highlighted the potential of Jammu & Kashmir as a formidable force in Indian domestic cricket.

With this victory, Jammu & Kashmir will now advance to the semi-finals, where they will look to build on this momentum. The team has shown great determination and skill throughout the tournament, and with Nabi leading from the front, they are poised to make an impact in the upcoming matches. This win marks a new chapter in Jammu & Kashmir's cricketing history, as they aim to continue their impressive run in the Ranji Trophy.
