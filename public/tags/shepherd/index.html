Hetmyer Heroics, Shepherd Hat-Trick Highlight West Indies' Opening Day Win Over Scotland

In a thrilling encounter at the picturesque Edgbaston, the West Indies showcased their cricketing prowess with a commanding victory over Scotland in their opening match of the ICC Cricket World Cup Qualifiers. The match, held on a sun-drenched day, saw Shimron Hetmyer steal the spotlight with a blistering century while Romario Shepherd’s exceptional bowling performance, which included a hat-trick, further solidified the Caribbean side’s dominance. Batting first, the West Indies posted a formidable total of 320 runs, largely due to Hetmyer’s explosive innings of 135 runs off just 98 balls. Hetmyer, known for his aggressive style, struck 12 fours and 6 sixes, displaying both power and finesse as he anchored the innings. His partnership with Shai Hope, who contributed a steady 65, was instrumental in setting a challenging target for the Scots.
