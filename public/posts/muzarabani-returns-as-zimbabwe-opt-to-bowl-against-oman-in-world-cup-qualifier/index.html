Muzarabani Returns as Zimbabwe Opt to Bowl Against Oman in World Cup Qualifier

Zimbabwe's Tendai Muzarabani makes a comeback as they choose to bowl first against Oman in the World Cup Qualifier match.

By BalleWale News Desk • February 9, 2026 • 2 min read

In a crucial World Cup Qualifier match held at the Al Amerat Cricket Ground, Zimbabwe won the toss and opted to bowl first against Oman. The decision came as a strategic move to leverage the morning conditions, which are often conducive to seam bowling. Zimbabwe’s captain, Craig Ervine, expressed confidence in his bowling unit, particularly with the return of fast bowler Tendai Muzarabani, who has been sidelined due to injury for several months.

Muzarabani’s inclusion in the playing XI is a significant boost for Zimbabwe. The tall pacer, known for his ability to extract bounce and movement, adds depth to an already potent bowling attack. His previous performances in international cricket have showcased his capability to take crucial wickets at key moments, making him a pivotal figure in Zimbabwe’s campaign. The team management has been keen to reintegrate him into the squad, especially given the importance of this match in their quest for World Cup qualification.

On the other hand, Oman, who are relatively new to the international cricketing scene, will be looking to make an impact against a more established Zimbabwean side. With a blend of experienced players and exciting young talent, Oman has shown resilience in their previous matches. They will aim to set a competitive total, leveraging the batting-friendly conditions expected later in the day. The team will rely on their top-order batsmen to provide a solid foundation, allowing the middle and lower order to capitalize on any scoring opportunities.

As the match progresses, all eyes will be on the performance of Muzarabani and the Zimbabwean bowlers, who will need to strike early to put pressure on the Omani batting lineup. The outcome of this match not only holds significance for both teams in the context of the tournament but also serves as a platform for players to showcase their skills on an international stage. With the stakes high, fans can expect an exciting contest as Zimbabwe aims to assert their dominance while Oman seeks to continue their impressive run in the qualifiers.
