Ellis Backs Australia's Pace Depth to Cover Big-Name Absences Ahead of World Cup

By BalleWale Editorial Team • Feb 9, 2026 • 2 min read

As Australia gears up for the ICC Cricket World Cup, fast bowler Nathan Ellis has expressed confidence in the team’s pace depth to compensate for the absence of key players due to injuries. With the tournament set to commence soon, the Australian squad has faced significant challenges, including the loss of prominent bowlers such as Pat Cummins and Josh Hazlewood. However, Ellis believes that the emerging talent within the squad is more than capable of stepping up to the occasion.

Ellis, who has been a standout performer in domestic cricket, highlighted the importance of adaptability and resilience in the face of adversity. “Injuries are part of the game, and while we will miss our experienced bowlers, we have a strong group of fast bowlers ready to take on the responsibility,” he stated. The Australian selectors have opted to include several young and dynamic bowlers in the squad, aiming to blend experience with youth to maintain a competitive edge.

The depth of Australia’s pace attack has been a topic of discussion among cricket analysts, especially given the rigorous nature of the World Cup schedule. With matches played in quick succession, the ability to rotate bowlers and manage workloads will be crucial. Ellis emphasized that the younger bowlers have been honing their skills and are eager to make their mark on the international stage. “We’ve all been training hard, and there’s a real sense of excitement within the group. Everyone is ready to seize their opportunity,” he added.

As Australia prepares to defend its title, the focus will be on how well the pace bowlers can adapt to different conditions and challenges presented during the tournament. With Ellis leading the charge, the Australian bowling unit aims to prove that their depth can withstand the pressures of high-stakes cricket. The World Cup promises to be a testing ground for these emerging talents, and the cricketing world will be watching closely to see if they can rise to the occasion.
