Straker and Swepson Shine as Queensland Overcome Tasmania in Domestic Shield Clash

By BalleWale Editorial Team ‚Ä¢ Feb 8, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 2 min read

In a thrilling encounter at the Gabba, Queensland’s standout performances from all-rounder Sam Straker and spinner Mitchell Swepson propelled them to a convincing victory over Tasmania, setting the stage for a potential Sheffield Shield final preview. Queensland’s 8-wicket triumph not only showcased their depth and resilience but also highlighted Tasmania’s struggles in the competition.

Straker was the star of the show, scoring a vital 85 runs in the second innings, which helped Queensland chase down Tasmania’s target of 195 runs with relative ease. His innings was characterized by a blend of aggression and composure, as he struck nine boundaries and built crucial partnerships with the lower order. Meanwhile, Swepson’s exceptional bowling performance, which included figures of 4 for 39 in the first innings and 3 for 32 in the second, dismantled Tasmania’s batting line-up, leaving them reeling at crucial junctures of the match.

Tasmania, who had opted to bat first, struggled to find momentum and were bowled out for just 190 runs in their first innings. Their top order faltered against a disciplined Queensland bowling attack, with only a few batsmen managing to reach double figures. The second innings saw a slight improvement, but Swepson’s spin proved too much for the Tasmanian batsmen, who failed to build any significant partnerships.

With this victory, Queensland has solidified their position in the Sheffield Shield standings, while Tasmania will need to regroup quickly as they look to bounce back in the upcoming matches. The clash between these two teams may very well be a precursor to the Shield final, as both sides aim for a strong finish to the season. As the competition heats up, all eyes will be on how both teams navigate the remaining fixtures, with Queensland emerging as strong contenders for the title.
