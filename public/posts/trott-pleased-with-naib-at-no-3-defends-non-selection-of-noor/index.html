Trott Pleased with Naib at No. 3, Defends Non-Selection of Noor

By BalleWale Editorial Team ‚Ä¢ Feb 8, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 2 min read

In a recent press conference, Afghanistan’s head coach, Andy Trott, expressed his satisfaction with the decision to position Gulbadin Naib at the crucial No. 3 batting spot during the ongoing World Cup 2023. Trott highlighted Naib’s experience and adaptability as key factors that influenced this strategic choice. The coach noted that Naib’s ability to anchor the innings while also accelerating the scoring rate made him an ideal candidate for the role, especially in high-pressure matches.

Trott’s comments come in the wake of criticism regarding the non-selection of promising young talent Noor Ahmad, a decision that has raised eyebrows among fans and analysts alike. When questioned about Noor’s absence from the squad, Trott defended the selection process, emphasizing the importance of experience in the current tournament. “While Noor is undoubtedly a talented player, we felt that the squad needed a blend of youth and experience to navigate the challenges of the World Cup,” Trott stated. He further elaborated that the decision was based on performance metrics and the players’ readiness to handle the intensity of international cricket at this level.

The coach’s remarks reflect a broader strategy aimed at balancing the team’s lineup with seasoned players who can provide stability in critical situations. Naib’s role at No. 3 has proven vital in recent matches, allowing Afghanistan to build solid partnerships and set competitive targets. Trott reiterated that the team’s focus remains on maximizing their potential and learning from each game, regardless of the selection debates that may arise.

As Afghanistan continues its campaign in the World Cup, the decisions made by the coaching staff will be closely scrutinized. Trott’s confidence in Naib and his rationale for Noor’s exclusion indicate a commitment to a strategic approach that prioritizes team dynamics and performance over individual accolades. With several matches still to play, fans will be eager to see how these decisions impact Afghanistan’s performance on the world stage.
