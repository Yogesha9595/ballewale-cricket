Suryakumar's 84* Helps India Overcome USA Scare in T20 Clash

By BalleWale Editorial Team • Feb 7, 2026 • 2 min read

In a thrilling T20 encounter held at the Central Broward Regional Park in Lauderhill, Florida, Suryakumar Yadav’s unbeaten 84 runs guided India to a narrow victory against the USA. The match, which showcased the growing popularity of cricket in the United States, saw India chasing a challenging target of 165 runs set by the hosts. Despite a shaky start, Yadav’s stellar performance ensured that India crossed the finish line with just two balls to spare.

India’s innings began on a rocky note as they lost their opener early, with the score at just 15 runs. The USA bowlers, led by their captain and star performer, exhibited impressive discipline and skill, putting the Indian batsmen under pressure. However, Suryakumar Yadav, known for his explosive batting style, anchored the innings with a masterclass in shot selection and timing. His innings included six boundaries and three sixes, as he deftly maneuvered the ball around the field, finding gaps and rotating the strike effectively.

The USA’s bowling attack, which included some promising young talents, managed to keep the Indian batsmen in check for much of the innings. Yet, as the required run rate climbed, Yadav shifted gears, taking calculated risks that ultimately paid off. His partnership with Hardik Pandya, who contributed a crucial 34 runs, was pivotal in stabilizing the innings during a difficult phase. The duo’s efforts not only brought India back into the game but also set the stage for a thrilling finish.

In the end, India’s victory was a testament to their resilience and depth in batting. The match drew attention not only for the cricketing action but also for the growing interest in the sport within the USA. With this win, India continues to bolster its reputation in T20 cricket, while the USA showcased their potential on the international stage. As the cricketing world watches, this match could be a significant step in the expansion of the game in non-traditional markets. The victory also serves as a reminder that no opponent should be taken lightly, regardless of their experience or ranking.
