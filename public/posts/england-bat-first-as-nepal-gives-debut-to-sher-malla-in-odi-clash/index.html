England Bat First as Nepal Gives Debut to Sher Malla in ODI Clash

In an exciting ODI match, England chooses to bat first while Nepal hands a debut to Sher Malla, showcasing emerging talent.

By BalleWale News Desk • February 8, 2026 • 2 min read

In a highly anticipated One Day International (ODI) match, England won the toss and elected to bat first against Nepal, who have handed a debut to promising young cricketer Sher Malla. The match, taking place at the Tribhuvan University International Cricket Ground in Kathmandu, marks a significant moment for Nepalese cricket as they continue to develop their talent on the international stage.

England, coming off a strong performance in their previous series, will be looking to set a competitive total. The team boasts a formidable batting lineup, featuring several players with extensive experience in international cricket. Captain Jos Buttler expressed confidence in his team’s ability to adapt to the conditions, which may favor spin, given the pitch’s history. The English batsmen will aim to build a solid foundation and put pressure on the Nepalese bowlers early in the innings.

On the other hand, Nepal’s decision to include Sher Malla in the playing XI underlines their commitment to nurturing young talent. Malla, who has shown promise in domestic circuits and age-group competitions, is expected to bring energy and fresh perspectives to the team. The Nepalese squad, while still developing, has shown resilience and skill in previous outings, and they will be looking to capitalize on any opportunities presented by the English batting order.

As the match unfolds, both teams will be keen to make their mark. England aims to assert their dominance in the format, while Nepal seeks to showcase their growth and potential on the world stage. This encounter not only highlights the competitive spirit of international cricket but also emphasizes the importance of developing young talent in the sport. Fans from both nations are eagerly watching, as this match promises to deliver exciting cricket and memorable moments.
