England Bat First; Nepal Hand Debut to Sher Malla in Thrilling Encounter

By BalleWale Editorial Team • Feb 8, 2026 • 2 min read

In a highly anticipated international fixture, England won the toss and elected to bat first against Nepal, who have handed a debut to promising young cricketer Sher Malla. The match, taking place at the Tribhuvan University International Cricket Ground in Kirtipur, marks a significant moment for Nepalese cricket as they continue to develop their talent on the global stage.

England, coming off a strong performance in their previous series, aimed to set a challenging target for the hosts. The decision to bat first was influenced by the conditions, which appeared to favor batting early on. England’s top order, featuring some of the most formidable batsmen in the world, was expected to capitalize on the pitch’s initial offering. Meanwhile, the Nepalese team, buoyed by the inclusion of Malla, looked to make a statement against their more experienced opponents.

Sher Malla’s debut is a landmark achievement for Nepalese cricket, as he becomes part of a growing list of players representing the nation on an international platform. His inclusion in the squad reflects the team’s commitment to nurturing young talent and building a competitive side. Malla, who has shown promise in domestic circuits, will be looking to make an impact in his first outing, contributing both with bat and ball.

As the match progresses, the focus will be on how England’s batsmen adapt to the conditions and how Nepal’s bowlers, led by their experienced campaigners, respond to the challenge. The game promises to be an exciting contest, showcasing the evolving landscape of international cricket, where teams like Nepal are steadily making their mark. Fans from both nations are eagerly watching, hoping for a display of skill and sportsmanship that transcends the boundaries of the game.
