Doggett's Season Over as Rain Frustrates New South Wales

New South Wales faces a disappointing end to the season as rain washes out crucial matches, marking the end of Doggett's campaign.

By BalleWale News Desk • February 8, 2026 • 2 min read

New South Wales’ hopes of a successful conclusion to their domestic season have been dashed as rain played a significant role in disrupting crucial matches. The inclement weather has not only affected the team’s performance but has also brought an early end to the season for promising fast bowler, Chris Doggett. The 25-year-old, who has been a standout performer for the side, will now miss the remainder of the competition due to an unfortunate injury sustained earlier in the season.

The rain-affected fixtures have left New South Wales in a precarious position in the tournament standings. With several matches needing to be played to determine playoff positions, the inability to take the field has frustrated players and management alike. The team was looking to build momentum after a series of competitive performances, but the weather has thwarted their efforts, leaving them with limited opportunities to secure their place in the knockout stages.

Doggett’s absence will be felt keenly by New South Wales, as he has been a key figure in their bowling attack throughout the season. His ability to take wickets at crucial moments and maintain pressure on opposing batsmen has made him an invaluable asset to the squad. The team now faces the challenge of filling the void left by his departure, as they look to navigate the final stages of the tournament amidst the uncertainty brought on by the weather.

As the season draws to a close, New South Wales will be hoping for a swift return to form and fitness for their remaining players. The management is now tasked with evaluating the squad’s performance and planning for the future, as they aim to bounce back stronger in the next season. For now, however, the focus remains on weather conditions and the hope that the rain will relent, allowing the team to finish their campaign on a high note.
