Manchester United Owner Among Bidders to Buy RCB

Manchester United's owner is reportedly among the bidders interested in acquiring Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) in the IPL.

By BalleWale News Desk ‚Ä¢ February 9, 2026 ‚Ä¢ 2 min read

In a significant development within the Indian Premier League (IPL), reports have emerged that the owner of Manchester United, Avram Glazer, is among several bidders interested in acquiring the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB). This potential acquisition could mark a new chapter for the franchise, which has been a prominent name in the IPL since its inception in 2008 but is yet to clinch the coveted title.

The interest from Glazer, who is part of the Glazer family that owns Manchester United, highlights the increasing crossover between sports franchises across different leagues and countries. RCB, based in Bengaluru, has a passionate fan base and is known for its star-studded line-up, including international cricketing icons. The franchise has consistently performed well in the league stages but has faced challenges in the knockout rounds, leading to a growing demand for investment that could enhance its competitive edge.

The bidding process for RCB is expected to attract a range of investors, both from India and abroad, as the IPL continues to grow in stature and financial value. With the league’s lucrative broadcasting deals and sponsorship opportunities, acquiring a franchise like RCB could be seen as a strategic investment. The involvement of high-profile investors like Glazer could also bring additional attention to the league, further elevating its global profile.

As the bidding process unfolds, fans and analysts alike will be watching closely to see how this potential acquisition could impact RCB’s future direction and performance in the IPL. The franchise’s management has yet to make any official comments regarding the bids, but the prospect of new ownership could bring fresh strategies and resources aimed at finally securing that elusive IPL title.
