Manchester United Owner Among Bidders for RCB and RR in IPL Auction

Manchester United owner joins the bidding race for IPL franchises RCB and RR, signaling a new era in Indian cricket.

By BalleWale News Desk • February 9, 2026 • 2 min read

In a significant development for the Indian Premier League (IPL), the owner of Manchester United, Avram Glazer, has emerged as one of the bidders in the auction for two prominent franchises: Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) and Rajasthan Royals (RR). This move marks a notable intersection between football and cricket, as Glazer’s involvement could potentially reshape the business landscape of the IPL, which has become one of the most lucrative cricket leagues in the world.

The bidding process for RCB and RR has attracted considerable attention, not only due to the franchises’ historical performances but also because of their substantial fan bases. RCB, known for its star-studded lineup including the likes of Virat Kohli and AB de Villiers in previous seasons, has yet to secure an IPL title, making it a tantalizing prospect for any investor. On the other hand, RR, the inaugural champions of the IPL in 2008, has a rich legacy and a dedicated following, which adds to its appeal.

Avram Glazer’s interest in these franchises is seen as part of a broader strategy to diversify his investment portfolio, especially in the realm of sports. The IPL has consistently attracted global attention and investment, with franchises often valued in the hundreds of millions of dollars. Glazer’s experience in managing a high-profile sports team like Manchester United could provide valuable insights into the operations of an IPL franchise, potentially enhancing its competitiveness and commercial success.

As the auction approaches, stakeholders within the cricketing community are keenly observing how this bidding will unfold. The involvement of high-profile investors like Glazer not only highlights the financial viability of the IPL but also underscores the growing global interest in Indian cricket. The outcome of this bidding process could have significant implications for the future of RCB and RR, as well as for the overall dynamics of the IPL. With the league continuing to expand its reach and influence, the arrival of new investors could signal a transformative period for the franchises involved and the league as a whole.
