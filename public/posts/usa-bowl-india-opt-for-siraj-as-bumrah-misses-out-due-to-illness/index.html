USA Bowl, India Opt for Siraj as Bumrah Misses Out Due to Illness

India's bowling lineup sees a change as Jasprit Bumrah is sidelined with illness, paving the way for Mohammed Siraj in the latest match against the USA.

By BalleWale News Desk • February 7, 2026 • 2 min read

In a significant development ahead of their latest World Cup fixture, India has announced that pace spearhead Jasprit Bumrah will miss the match against the USA due to illness. In his absence, the Indian selectors have opted to include Mohammed Siraj in the playing XI, a decision that underscores the team’s depth in bowling options as they look to maintain their strong performance in the tournament.

Bumrah, who has been a pivotal figure in India’s bowling attack, was reported unwell just hours before the match, prompting the team management to make a swift change. Siraj, who has been in impressive form, particularly in the ODI format, will be looking to seize this opportunity to showcase his skills on the world stage. The 29-year-old has been a consistent performer for India, and his ability to swing the ball both ways could prove crucial against a USA side that is relatively new to the World Cup environment.

The USA, on the other hand, will be keen to capitalize on India’s adjustment in their bowling lineup. With a mix of youthful exuberance and experienced players, the USA team aims to put forth a competitive challenge. Their strategy will likely focus on building partnerships and capitalizing on any early breakthroughs that Siraj and the Indian bowlers might offer. As the match unfolds, all eyes will be on Siraj to see if he can fill the significant void left by Bumrah and lead the Indian attack effectively.

As the teams prepare to take the field, the stakes are high for both sides. India aims to solidify their position in the tournament and continue their quest for World Cup glory, while the USA seeks to make a mark in their inaugural World Cup appearance. The absence of Bumrah adds an intriguing layer to the match, and fans will be eager to see how the Indian bowling unit adapts to the change. With Siraj stepping in, the stage is set for an exciting encounter.
