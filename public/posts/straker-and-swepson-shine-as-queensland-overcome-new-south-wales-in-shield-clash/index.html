Straker and Swepson Shine as Queensland Overcome New South Wales in Shield Clash

By BalleWale Editorial Team • Feb 8, 2026 • 2 min read

In a thrilling encounter at the Gabba, Queensland’s standout performances from all-rounder Sam Straker and spinner Mitchell Swepson propelled them to a decisive victory over New South Wales, setting the stage for a potential Sheffield Shield final preview. The match concluded with Queensland securing a 142-run win, showcasing their depth and resilience as they aim for the title.

Straker’s all-round capabilities were on full display as he contributed significantly with both bat and ball. Scoring a crucial 75 runs in the first innings, he helped Queensland post a competitive total of 307. His contributions did not stop there; he also took three wickets in the second innings, playing a pivotal role in dismantling New South Wales’ batting lineup. Straker’s ability to perform under pressure was evident, as he consistently found ways to break partnerships and maintain the momentum for his team.

On the other hand, Swepson’s spin bowling proved to be a game-changer. He claimed a remarkable five-wicket haul in the second innings, effectively stifling New South Wales’ chase. His control and variation troubled the opposition batsmen, leading to crucial breakthroughs at key moments. Swepson’s performance not only highlighted his skills as a leading spinner in domestic cricket but also reinforced Queensland’s bowling attack as one of the most formidable in the competition.

New South Wales, despite a valiant effort, struggled to build substantial partnerships throughout the match. Their first innings total of 215 set them back significantly, and although they showed signs of recovery in the second innings, they ultimately fell short, bowled out for 150. Queensland’s victory not only boosts their confidence heading into the latter stages of the Sheffield Shield but also raises the stakes for the upcoming matches, as both teams eye a spot in the final. With the season progressing, the performances of Straker and Swepson could be pivotal in Queensland’s quest for the championship, while New South Wales will need to regroup and reassess their strategies in the coming fixtures.
