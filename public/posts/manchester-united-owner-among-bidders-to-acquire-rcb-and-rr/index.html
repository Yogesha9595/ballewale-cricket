Manchester United Owner Among Bidders to Acquire RCB and RR

By BalleWale Editorial Team • Feb 9, 2026 • 2 min read

In a significant development in the Indian Premier League (IPL), reports have emerged that Manchester United owner Avram Glazer is among the bidders looking to acquire the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) and Rajasthan Royals (RR). The potential acquisition of these franchises could reshape the ownership landscape of the IPL, which has been a cornerstone of Indian cricket since its inception in 2008.

The Glazer family, known for their controversial ownership of Manchester United, has reportedly expressed interest in diversifying their sports portfolio by investing in cricket. This move aligns with a broader trend of cross-sport investments, where owners of major football clubs are looking to tap into the lucrative world of cricket. The IPL, with its massive viewership and commercial success, presents an attractive opportunity for investors.

The bidding process for RCB and RR is expected to attract a variety of investors, both domestic and international. RCB, known for its passionate fan base and star-studded lineup, has yet to secure an IPL title, making it an appealing target for those looking to make a mark in the league. On the other hand, RR, the inaugural champions of the IPL, have a rich history and a strong following, further increasing their value in the eyes of potential buyers.

As the IPL continues to grow in stature and profitability, the involvement of high-profile investors like Avram Glazer could lead to increased competition and investment in the league. The outcome of this bidding process will not only impact the franchises involved but could also set a precedent for future ownership dynamics within the IPL. The cricketing community will be keenly watching how this situation unfolds, as it may herald a new era for these franchises and the league as a whole.
